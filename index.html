Jason Edelman

Developer. Social cybernetics for the green transition.

I work at the intersection of software, systems thinking, and sustainability. I was a software engineer from 2004 to 2014, and my background in environmental education — first as a volunteer, then as a camp counselor — shapes how I think about collective behavior and change. For the past five years my wife and I ran a vegetarian restaurant in the South; we recently sold it.

Projects

  • Power Explained

    An educational resource examining power structures, capitalism, and commons-based alternatives. The argument: systemic problems are design features, and meaningful change happens by building alternatives.

  • Ghent Streets

    A community advocacy campaign for zoning reform in Norfolk, Virginia's HC-G2 historic district — to allow street-level commercial activity by right and restore pedestrian vitality to the neighborhood.

  • Mithlond

    Community compute for Hampton Roads. Applying a commons framework to AI-era data center infrastructure: who owns it, who bears the environmental costs, and whether local communities see structural economic benefit.
