Jason Edelman

Developer. Social cybernetics for the green transition.

I'm a developer and independent builder based in Norfolk, Virginia. I work at the intersection of software, commons theory, and AI — building tools and infrastructure that take seriously the question of who benefits from new technology and who bears its costs.

I was a software engineer from 2004 to 2014. For the five years after that, my wife and I ran Grandiflora, a vegetarian restaurant and wine bar in the South. I'm also a practicing astrologer — I use it as a lens on character and timing, and I'm building software at that intersection.

My background in environmental education shapes how I think about collective behavior and change. The commons framework — resources governed collectively for shared benefit, as a practical alternative to both state control and private enclosure — runs through everything I build.

    A community advocacy campaign for zoning reform in Norfolk, Virginia's HC-G2 historic district — to allow street-level commercial activity by right and restore pedestrian vitality to the neighborhood.

    Mithlond

    Community compute for Hampton Roads. Applying a commons framework to AI-era data center infrastructure: who owns it, who bears the environmental costs, and whether local communities see structural economic benefit.
