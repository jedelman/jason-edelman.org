Nine Pieces in One Session

April 6, 2026

Series VI — "Enclosure of the Mind" — covers the twentieth century's parallel project: the enclosure not of physical resources but of attention, desire, consciousness, and language. Nine pieces, all written and through two editorial passes in a single long day.

The arc runs from Wilhelm Reich in the 1930s through Hardt and Negri after September 2001. Reich: how authoritarian politics finds its substrate in bodily suppression — the mass psychology problem isn't why people rebel but why they don't. Gramsci in Turin in the 1930s, situating him historically rather than theoretically — the factory council movement, the Southern Question, passive revolution as the mode by which capital absorbs challenge without conceding anything. Shannon and Simondon in the 1950s, reading information theory against the emergence of mass media. Debord and May '68: the spectacle as a social relation, not a collection of images, and détournement as the counter-practice. Psychedelics and MKULTRA: the government's attempt to weaponize altered states alongside the counterculture's attempt to liberate them — and the three-phase closure that followed. Baudrillard's consumer society: sign-value replacing use-value, desire managed through the system of objects. The 1980s drug war and synthesizers: how hybrid musical forms composed from excluded communities became commons built in the gaps of enclosure, and how the 808, 303, and SL-1200 found their associated milieu precisely because the mainstream had no use for them. Hardt and Negri and the Global War on Terror: immaterial labor as the new enclosure, the Patriot Act as an Enclosure Act for civil space.

After the first draft, two editorial passes: one to move every section's thesis to the opening sentence rather than burying it, and one to cut repetition — every argument stated once, clearly. No restating. The condensing pass removed several hundred words across the nine pieces without losing any load-bearing content.

The session ran long. The series is live at power-explained.jason-edelman.org.
