Launch Day: jason-edelman.org, Shannon, and Gödel

March 17, 2026

Three things shipped today.

This site launched — plain HTML/CSS, Netlify auto-deploy from GitHub, intentionally minimal. Before writing a word of content, I wrote a CLAUDE.md with explicit anti-hallucination guardrails: no invented biographical facts, no placeholder project descriptions, no social handles that haven't been verified. The rule applies to all my repos now. If I can't verify it, it doesn't go in.

On power-explained, I wrote a piece on Claude Shannon — specifically the argument that information channels are commons infrastructure. Bell Labs, where Shannon worked, is a useful case study: the AT&T consent decree barred it from competing in computing, so it had no product roadmap. What it produced instead, without one, was the transistor, Unix, C, and information theory itself. Divestiture in 1984 destroyed the institutional structure that made that possible. The parallel to open protocols is direct: HTTP, HTML, TCP/IP were all built as commons before any enclosure was complete. The platform era is colonization of a neighborhood someone else built.

The other piece extended the Gödel thinker page with two new sections connecting his incompleteness theorems to Deleuze and Guattari's concept of lines of flight. The argument: Gödel proved formally that every sufficiently powerful system generates true statements it cannot prove within itself. D&G argue that every social system generates lines of flight — tendencies and possibilities the system cannot contain. The parallel isn't metaphorical. Both are describing the same structural feature of complex systems: the outside is guaranteed. Hegemony's claimed completeness is always a lie, in the same sense that a formal system's claimed completeness is always false.

Gödel is now the project's logician — the mathematician who proved that the outside is structural, not contingent on hope.
